The problem

You already have feedback: star ratings, reviews, maybe a post-visit survey.

The problem isn’t lack of feedback. It’s that none of it explains why customers behave as they do.

“Everything looks fine — but something isn’t clicking.”

Scores flatten the story

Ratings tell you what guests felt, but not what created that feeling.

Reviews are incomplete

They over-index on extremes and miss the quiet reasons people don’t return.

Surveys optimize for measurement

What Anthrosight does

Anthrosight runs short, post-visit reflections with recent customers and synthesizes what they say into a clear, readable insight memo.

Instead of asking customers to rate their experience, we ask them to briefly reflect on it. What comes out is: expectation gaps, emotional inflection points, and the language customers use when describing you to others—things ratings and surveys systematically miss.

What customers are actually asked

Every reflection follows the same simple structure:

  • “Thinking back on your visit, what stood out most to you?”
  • “Was that what you expected going in — or different in some way?”
  • “Was there a moment where how you felt about the experience changed?”
  • “If you were telling a friend about this place, what would you say?”

Optional final prompt (time permitting): “Anything you wish had been different?”
No surveys. No demographics. No forced completeness.

The deliverable: the memo

The memo is the product.

It includes:

  • Expectation gaps — what guests assumed vs what they experienced
  • Emotional inflection points — where and why sentiment shifted (with direct quotes)
  • The story guests tell — the language they use to describe you to others
  • What might be worth trying — one or two small changes suggested by patterns (starting points, not prescriptions)

If reflections don’t yield usable insight, we’ll say so—and you won’t be charged the full fee.
