How a listening sprint works

1

Pick a moment

After checkout, after a meal, after a service visit — one experience type at a time.

2

Invite guests to reflect

Add a single line + link to the email you already send. No new tools required.

3

We listen (no surveys)

Guests share short narratives. We probe gently for expectations, shifts, and meaning.

4

You get a memo you’ll read

A concise synthesis: what mattered, what didn’t, and the specific moments to fix.

Typical pilot: 10–25 completed reflections over ~2 weeks.

Example: what a memo uncovers

“The food was great — but the pacing made it feel like we were being rushed the whole time.”
Common pattern we see: guest satisfaction hinges on expectation gaps (pacing, noise, arrival, communication), not just “quality.”
Insights you don’t get from a rating:
  • Which moments guests remember first (arrival, greeting, wait).
  • Where emotion shifted (from excited → anxious → resigned).
  • What guests tell friends (the “story”), not the score.
  • What “fine” actually means — and why it doesn’t convert to loyalty.
